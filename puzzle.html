Gamify (Verb)

  • Definition: To apply game mechanics or principles to a task, system, or process.
  • Example Usage: "We need to gamify our employee training program to make it more engaging."
  • Applications: Highlights the action of transforming non-game contexts into engaging, game-like experiences.

Gamified (Adjective)

  • Definition: Describing a system, process, or activity that has been enhanced with game-like elements.
  • Example Usage: "The app's gamified interface keeps users hooked with rewards and challenges."
  • Applications: Used to describe something that has already undergone the process of gamification.

Metagamification (Noun)

  • Definition: The gamification of gamification itself, where systems compete on how effectively they gamify their processes.
  • Example Usage: "In metagamification, platforms compare their gamified elements to outdo one another."
  • Applications: A meta-level critique or analysis of gamification practices.

Degamification (Noun)

  • Definition: The process of removing or reducing gamified elements from a system.
  • Example Usage: "The app underwent degamification after users complained about overwhelming notifications."
  • Applications: Used when systems overuse or misuse gamification, requiring a rollback to simpler, less competitive structures.
